Health, Ethnicity & Race Analytics

In ancient Greek myth and religion, Hera is the goddess of women, marriage, family, and childbirth. Her Roman counterpart is Juno, who is also regarded as protectress of the community and responsible for imbuing both women and men with vital energy.

The purpose of this project is to analyze health disparities among various ethnic and racial groups in the United States, particularly as they relate to maternal, child, and family care. In addition, our aim is to help reconcile conflicting studies in the area of public health by enabling users to explore, replicate, and dispute findings found in peer-reviewed literature.

Maternal Mortality: Racial Bias vs. Delivery Method

 Data from the Centers of Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) show that Black women are 3 to 4 times more likely to die from pregnancy-related causes than White women, prompting potential legislation to fund implicit bias training for healthcare providers. This article examines whether the disparity in pregnancy-related deaths is because of racial bias or Black women disproportionately undergoing riskier delivery methods such as c-sections.
